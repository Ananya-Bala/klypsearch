# Amazon.com, Inc. — Q4 2024 Earnings Report

## Financial Highlights

Amazon reported quarterly net sales of $187.8 billion, up 10% year over year.
Operating income was $21.2 billion, up 61% year over year, and net income was
$20.0 billion. Diluted earnings per share were $1.86. Full-year operating cash flow
rose substantially as the company's cost-to-serve improvements compounded.

## AWS Performance

Amazon Web Services revenue was $28.8 billion, up 19% year over year, reaching an
annualized revenue run rate of more than $115 billion. AWS operating income was
$10.6 billion, with an operating margin of 36.9%. Management said AI is now a
multi-billion-dollar annualized revenue business within AWS and is growing at
triple-digit percentages year over year. The company highlighted demand for its
Trainium custom silicon and said capacity constraints, rather than demand, are
currently the main limiter on AI revenue growth.

## Advertising and Retail

Advertising services revenue grew 18% year over year to $17.3 billion, continuing
to be one of the company's fastest-growing and highest-margin segments. North
America segment sales grew 10% and International segment sales grew 8% on a
constant-currency basis. Management highlighted faster delivery speeds and a record
holiday shopping season.

## AI and Capital Investment

Management stated that the company plans to significantly increase capital
expenditures, primarily to support AI and AWS infrastructure, with much of the
spend directed toward data centers, custom chips, and networking. The CEO described
generative AI as a once-in-a-generation opportunity and said the company is
building AI capabilities across every major business unit, including its Bedrock
model platform, the Q assistant, and AI-powered shopping and logistics tools.

## Margins and Efficiency

Consolidated operating margin reached 11.3%, reflecting AWS strength, advertising
growth, and continued fulfillment network efficiency gains. Management reiterated a
focus on lowering the cost to serve while investing in long-term growth areas.

## Outlook

For the next quarter, Amazon guided to net sales growth in the high single digits
to low double digits year over year. Management cautioned that elevated capital
investment in AI infrastructure would weigh on near-term free cash flow but
described it as a justified investment given the scale of customer demand.
