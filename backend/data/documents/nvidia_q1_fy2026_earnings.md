# NVIDIA Corporation — Q1 FY2026 Earnings Report

## Financial Highlights

NVIDIA reported record quarterly revenue of $44.1 billion, up 69% year over year
and up 12% sequentially. Data Center revenue reached $39.1 billion, an all-time
high, driven by continued ramp of the Blackwell platform. Gross margin came in at
71.3% on a GAAP basis. Diluted earnings per share were $0.96, up 82% from the
prior year period.

## Management Commentary on AI Demand

Management stated that demand for AI infrastructure remains "extraordinary and
broad-based." The company emphasized that demand for Blackwell continues to
outstrip supply, and that hyperscale customers, sovereign AI initiatives, and
enterprise adopters are all expanding their AI compute footprints simultaneously.

On AI demand specifically, the CEO noted that the world is in the early innings of
a multi-year build-out of AI factories, and that inference demand is accelerating
faster than expected as reasoning models and agentic workloads move into
production. The company said that every major cloud provider has communicated
plans to grow AI capacity through the next fiscal year, and that token generation
volumes across customer deployments have grown more than tenfold year over year.

Management added that demand signals are not limited to large language model
training. AI demand is broadening into recommendation systems, scientific
computing, robotics simulation, and physical AI, which the company believes
represents a durable, structural growth driver rather than a one-time surge.

## Data Center Segment

Data Center revenue of $39.1 billion grew 73% year over year. Networking revenue,
including Spectrum-X Ethernet and NVLink, contributed meaningfully as customers
scaled out multi-node training clusters. The company highlighted strong adoption
of its full-stack platform spanning GPUs, CPUs, networking, and software.

## Gaming and Other Segments

Gaming revenue was $3.8 billion, up 42% year over year, supported by the RTX 50
series launch. Professional Visualization revenue was $0.5 billion and Automotive
revenue was $0.6 billion, with automotive growth driven by self-driving platform
design wins.

## Outlook and Guidance

For Q2 FY2026, NVIDIA guided to revenue of approximately $45 billion, plus or
minus 2%. The company expects gross margin in the low-70s percent range as
Blackwell yields improve. Management reiterated confidence in sustained AI demand
and described its visibility into customer capacity plans as the strongest in the
company's history.

## Capital Return

NVIDIA returned $14.3 billion to shareholders during the quarter through share
repurchases and dividends, and the board authorized an additional $50 billion in
buyback capacity.
