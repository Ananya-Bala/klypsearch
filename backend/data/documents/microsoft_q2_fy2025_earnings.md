# Microsoft Corporation — Q2 FY2025 Earnings Report

## Financial Highlights

Microsoft reported quarterly revenue of $69.6 billion, up 12% year over year.
Operating income was $31.7 billion, up 17%, and diluted earnings per share were
$3.23. Microsoft Cloud revenue surpassed $40.9 billion, up 21% year over year.

## Intelligent Cloud and Azure

Intelligent Cloud revenue was $25.5 billion, up 19% year over year. Azure and
other cloud services revenue grew 31%, with approximately 13 points of that growth
attributable to AI services. Management said Azure AI demand continued to exceed
available capacity and that the company is accelerating data center investments to
close the gap. The company reiterated plans to spend approximately $80 billion on
AI-enabled data centers during the fiscal year.

## AI and Copilot Adoption

Management highlighted that the AI business has surpassed an annual revenue run
rate of $13 billion, up 175% year over year. Microsoft 365 Copilot seat counts
continued to expand across enterprise customers, and the company noted accelerating
adoption of GitHub Copilot and Copilot Studio agents. Management framed AI as the
primary driver of incremental cloud consumption and described it as the fastest
business in the company's history to reach this scale.

## Productivity and Business Processes

Productivity and Business Processes revenue was $29.4 billion, up 14%, driven by
Microsoft 365 commercial cloud and LinkedIn. More Personal Computing revenue was
$14.7 billion, roughly flat, with gaming and Windows OEM offsetting one another.

## Capital Expenditure and Margins

Capital expenditures, including finance leases, were $22.6 billion for the quarter,
predominantly directed toward cloud and AI infrastructure. Operating margin was
45%, with management noting that near-term margins reflect heavy AI investment that
is expected to generate returns as capacity is monetized.

## Outlook

For the next quarter, management guided to continued double-digit revenue growth,
with Azure growth expected to remain in a similar range as AI capacity comes
online in the second half of the fiscal year. The company expects capital
expenditure to remain elevated to meet AI demand.
