# Tesla, Inc. — Q4 2024 Earnings Report

## Financial Highlights

Tesla reported quarterly revenue of $25.7 billion, up 2% year over year. GAAP
operating income was $1.6 billion, with an operating margin of 6.2%. GAAP earnings
per share were $0.66. Full-year revenue reached $97.7 billion. The company ended
the quarter with $36.6 billion in cash and investments.

## Deliveries and Production

Tesla delivered approximately 495,000 vehicles in the quarter and produced roughly
459,000 vehicles. Full-year deliveries were approximately 1.79 million vehicles.
Management noted that average selling prices declined year over year due to pricing
actions and financing incentives intended to sustain demand in a higher interest
rate environment.

## Energy Generation and Storage

Energy storage deployments reached a record 11.0 GWh in the quarter. Energy
generation and storage revenue grew sharply year over year, and management
identified the energy business as one of the fastest-growing and highest-margin
parts of the company. Megapack production at the company's dedicated factory
continued to ramp.

## Autonomy, AI, and Robotics

Management devoted significant commentary to artificial intelligence. The company
reiterated its roadmap for supervised full self-driving and stated that it expects
to launch unsupervised autonomy and a paid ride-hailing service in select markets
during the year. Management framed real-world AI, the in-house Dojo training
program, and the Optimus humanoid robot program as central to the company's
long-term value, and said Optimus pilot production is targeted to begin during the
year. The company emphasized that its vehicle fleet provides a unique real-world
data advantage for training autonomy models.

## Margins and Cost

Automotive gross margin excluding regulatory credits remained under pressure from
pricing, though management highlighted ongoing cost per vehicle reductions in
manufacturing. The company said a more affordable model is planned to begin
production in the first half of the year.

## Outlook

Management guided to a return to vehicle volume growth in the coming year, supported
by new model launches and lower-cost vehicles, while cautioning that the business
remains sensitive to interest rates and macroeconomic conditions. The company
expects the energy storage business to continue growing faster than the vehicle
business.
